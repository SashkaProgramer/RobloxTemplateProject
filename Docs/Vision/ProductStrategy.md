# Product Strategy

## Positioning

The project should sit between social survival and extraction-lite rather than trying to become a hardcore tactical shooter or a broad MMO-style survival game.

## Strategic Advantages

- Clear visual fantasy: a cursed night train.
- Strong session framing: one run, one route, one extraction climax.
- Built-in social conflict: not everyone can escape.
- Expandable live-ops surface through new wagons, events, loot sets, and cosmetic themes.

## Retention Strategy

- Strong early readability and tension.
- A repeatable match arc with uncertain outcomes.
- Valuable rare loot that changes player behavior.
- Fast re-queue loop after death or match end.

## Monetization Strategy

Prioritize:

- Character cosmetics
- Mask, lantern, and outfit variants
- Nameplates, titles, extraction effects
- Seasonal pass

Avoid for MVP:

- Hard pay-to-win power
- Competitive advantages that obviously break trust

## Virality Strategy

Clip-worthy moments should come from:

- betrayal at extraction
- last-second escapes
- players causing panic through sabotage
- rare loot discoveries followed by social fallout
